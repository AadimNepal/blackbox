Mentors & Researchers

8 members. Interests span medical AI, interpretability, and trustworthy deployment.

Mentor

Aadim Nepal

Aadim founded and leads Team Black Box. He's spent the last few years thinking about what happens when we hand important decisions to models nobody fully understands — and decided the fix starts with a team that actually opens the box instead of trusting the label on it. He mentors across every thread, from dataset design to the final audit checklist.

Peer Mentor

Ashok Timsina

Ashok keeps the lab honest. As peer mentor, he co-designs experiments with each researcher and makes sure every result survives a second look before anyone gets excited about it — new random seed, new test set, no exceptions. He's especially focused on making the team's Grad-CAM pipeline something other labs could actually reuse.

Medical AI, Bias

Yash Shah

Yash spends his time trying to trick chest X-ray models into revealing their shortcuts. His project injects tiny synthetic markers into training images to see how quickly a model abandons real medical reasoning for an easier pattern — so far, the answer has been uncomfortably fast.

Kripesh built a CNN completely from scratch — no pretrained weights, no shortcuts — because he wanted to understand exactly what happens between an image going in and a diagnosis coming out.

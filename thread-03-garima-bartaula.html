Do Models See Like Doctors?

Medical Imaging · Interpretability · Trustworthy AI
TL;DR

We compare model attention maps with clinician eye-tracking / bounding boxes.

The Problem

Interpretability should be human-aligned. If a model looks at background, clinicians won't trust it even if accuracy is high.

Our Approach

We collect 50 chest X-rays with radiologist annotated regions. Compute IoU between Grad-CAM thresholded mask and clinical annotation. Also run a small survey with 2 medical students rating 'does this focus make sense?'

What We Found

Mean IoU is only 0.31. In 40% cases, model focuses on clavicle or diaphragm, not opacity. Students rated 60% of maps as 'not clinically meaningful'. Gap between accuracy and alignment is real.

Why It Matters / Open Questions

If models rely on shortcuts, high accuracy is misleading. Next: test with external hospital data, compare Grad-CAM vs Score-CAM, and build a simple audit checklist clinicians can use before deployment.

References

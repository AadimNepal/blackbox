Shortcut Learning in Chest X-rays

Medical Imaging · Interpretability · Trustworthy AI
TL;DR

Models can achieve high accuracy by learning hospital markers (like text overlays) instead of actual pathology.

The Problem

Chest X-ray models often show excellent test accuracy in papers but fail in new hospitals. We suspected they learn spurious correlations — e.g., a specific hospital's portable X-ray machine adds a white border that correlates with pneumonia.

Our Approach

We created a synthetic dataset where we add a small colored dot correlated with disease label. Train a ResNet-18, then visualize with Grad-CAM. The model quickly learns to look at the dot, not the lung.

What We Found

When dot correlation = 0.9, model focuses 80% of Grad-CAM mass on the dot area. Remove dot at test time → accuracy drops from 91% to 62%. This demonstrates how easy shortcuts are.

Why It Matters / Open Questions

If models rely on shortcuts, high accuracy is misleading. Next: test with external hospital data, compare Grad-CAM vs Score-CAM, and build a simple audit checklist clinicians can use before deployment.

References

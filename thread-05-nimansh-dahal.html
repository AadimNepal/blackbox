When Interpretability Fails

Medical Imaging · Interpretability · Trustworthy AI
TL;DR

Cataloguing failure modes where Grad-CAM looks plausible but prediction is wrong.

The Problem

Good looking heatmap does not equal correct prediction. We need failure taxonomy.

Our Approach

We collect 30 failure cases: model predicts correctly but looks at wrong region, predicts wrongly but heatmap looks right, and heatmap is diffuse. Create 3 categories and analyze.

Original Image
e.g., chest X-ray
Grad-CAM Heatmap
model focus
# gradcam.py — pseudocode import torch from pytorch_grad_cam import GradCAM cam = GradCAM(model=model, target_layers=[model.layer4]) grayscale_cam = cam(input_tensor=image) show_cam_on_image(image, grayscale_cam)

What We Found

Most interesting failure: model predicts pneumonia correctly by looking at chest tube (treatment marker), not disease. Heatmap is sharp and convincing, but reasoning is wrong — 'right for wrong reasons'. This is dangerous for deployment.

Why It Matters / Open Questions

If models rely on shortcuts, high accuracy is misleading. Next: test with external hospital data, compare Grad-CAM vs Score-CAM, and build a simple audit checklist clinicians can use before deployment.

References

Selvaraju et al. Grad-CAM, Geirhos et al. Shortcut Learning, Adebayo et al. Sanity Checks.

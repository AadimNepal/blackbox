Building a Simple CNN from Scratch

Medical Imaging · Interpretability · Trustworthy AI
TL;DR

Understanding how a CNN builds features layer by layer — from edges to pathology.

The Problem

Before auditing large models, we need intuition for how learning happens. Many teammates haven't built CNNs from scratch.

Our Approach

We implement a 4-layer CNN in PyTorch, train on 1000 chest X-rays (pneumonia vs normal). Visualize filters, feature maps, and layer-wise Grad-CAM. Track what each layer detects.

What We Found

Layer1: edge detectors, Layer2: textures / rib patterns, Layer3-4: more abstract shapes. Grad-CAM becomes more focal with depth. Training longer makes maps sharper but also more prone to shortcuts if data has bias.

Why It Matters / Open Questions

If models rely on shortcuts, high accuracy is misleading. Next: test with external hospital data, compare Grad-CAM vs Score-CAM, and build a simple audit checklist clinicians can use before deployment.

References

Selvaraju et al. Grad-CAM, Geirhos et al. Shortcut Learning, Adebayo et al. Sanity Checks.
